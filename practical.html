Schedule

Workshops

The day starts with workshops: practicing rituals, rules, and mechanics. Time to meet your co-players and calibrate before the Game begins.

Act I — 12:00–15:00

Players begin dispersed across the city, each with their Familiar. Short pre-designed scenes help build understanding of other characters and start your first alliances. Minor Arcana become available for collection. Not yet the time to reveal your alignment.

Act II — 15:00–16:00 (lunch)

All Players and Familiars gather for a formal in-game lunch. Arcana collection pauses. You receive sealed envelopes with the Minor Arcana you collected in Act I and may begin using magic. Now is the time to feel which side you are drifting toward.

Act III — 16:00–20:00

Major Arcana enter play. The rules shift. From this point on, it becomes possible to eliminate another Player. Gather every grain of power you can.

Final act — 20:00

All players gather in one place. Everyone announces their side: Openers or Closers. All magical power is spent. This is where the story ends.

Food and costume

Food

One in-game meal is provided by the organizers for all players (Act II lunch). Alcohol during the game is strictly forbidden. If you have dietary requirements, note them in the sign-up form.

Costume

Your costume can contain subtle clues to your character's identity. The setting is modern, but a touch of the gothic or Victorian is welcome. Think mood over accuracy. October in Kraków can be cold, rainy, and unpredictable — dress warmly and be prepared for wind and rain. Comfort comes first.

Safety and accessibility

Physical demands

This game requires significant walking through Kraków over the course of a day. October weather will likely be cold and wet. You will be on your feet for most of the game. If you have mobility concerns, contact us before signing up.

Content

The game contains competitive dynamics, potential betrayal, trust manipulation, and power imbalance. Characters may be eliminated in Act III. The setting draws from gothic horror literature — themes include existential uncertainty, fate, and the nature of identity. There is no graphic violence or sexual content.
